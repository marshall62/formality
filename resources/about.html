About 4mality

4MALITY is a web-based tutoring system, designed to teach mathematical problem solving and test-taking to elementary school children.  Researchers from the School of Education, Department of Computer Science, and Center for Educational Software Development (CESD) at the University of Massachusetts Amherst are designing the system for use in public schools throughout Massachusetts.  Housed on the University’s server, 4MALITY is available free of charge to schools and families.

4MALITY is implemented with the Java servlet architecture supported by a relational database instance. The user interface utilizes html and other scripting languages. The system contains MCAS-like test questions, annotated with hints and feedback designed to teach a wide range of problem solving techniques to students of various levels of knowledge. 4MALITY’s grade level content includes third through fifth grade.

The MCAS (Massachusetts Comprehensive Assessment System) is a standardized test of math skills, presenting word problems that students must solve—in most instances, using multiple mathematical steps and number operations.  Required of all third, fourth, and fifth graders in the state’s public schools, MCAS is part of how Massachusetts complies with the U. S. Government’s No Child Left Behind (NCLB) law.

For each problem in 4MALITY, there are strategies and suggestions authored from a particular problem solving “point of view.”  Four “virtual coaches”—intended to capture the “character” of each problem solving approach—represent these “points of view.”  Each coach has an iconographic representation that helps students identify its problem solving approaches. 

  • Estella Explainer is a language coach who helps children understand the language and meaning of questions.
  • Chef Math Bear provides computational strategies (addition, subtraction, multiplication and division).
  • How-to-Hound presents strategic thinking clues (rounding, estimation, elimination of wrong answers).
  • Visual Vicuna offers ways to see problems and their solutions in pictures, charts and graphs.

These coaches represent a distillation of our collective experience about how elementary school students approach solving MCAS-style math word problems.

The History of 4mality

4th Grade MCAS Active Learning Intelligent Tutoring System (4mality) is a team-driven project aimed to increase 4th grade comprehension of the Massachusetts Comprehensive Assessment System (MCAS).

At the University of Massachusetts (Amherst), School of Education faculty members: Sharon Edwards, Bob Maloy, Oscar Loureiro and Gordon Anderson are working to introduce a trial of 4mality into elementary schools by June of 2010.
